Our story

A tradition passed through hands

Pieni puukkopaja — “The Little Knife Workshop” — was born from a lifelong passion for the Finnish wilderness. For over two decades, our artisan has been shaping puukko knives by hand in a small workshop nestled in Varkaus, in the heart of the Finnish Lakeland.

The puukko is more than a tool; it is a symbol of self-reliance, craftsmanship, and respect for nature. Every knife that leaves our workshop carries this heritage — from the responsibly sourced birch handle to the hand-ground carbon steel blade.

Craftsmanship

Materials & Process

Every puukko is born from carefully selected materials and time-honored techniques.

Finnish Birch

Handles carved from locally sourced curly birch, prized for its strength and beautiful grain patterns.

Carbon Steel

High-carbon steel blades forged and tempered for exceptional edge retention and easy field sharpening.

Hand Finishing

Every surface is finished by hand — sanded, polished, and oiled until it feels right in the palm.

Leather Sheath

A hand-stitched Finnish leather sheath completes every knife, embossed with the workshop mark.
